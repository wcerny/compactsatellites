Paper I: The Stellar Kinematics and Metallicites of
19 Extremely-Low-Mass Milky Way Satellites

(Cerny et al. 2026b, arxiv: )

Defining the Ultra-Faint Compact Satellites

We began by defining the population of Ultra-Faint Compact Satellites (UFCSs) based on four selections:

(1) Azimuthally-averaged half-light radii $r_{1/2} < 15$ pc
(2) Absolute magnitudes $M_V > -3.5$ ($M_* < 4000 \, \rm M_{\odot}$)
(3) Surface Brightness $\mu_V > -24$ mag/arcsec$^2$
(4) Galactocentric positions outside of the disk plane ($|Z| > 5$ kpc)

This yielded a total known population of 29 UFCSs, in addition to two classical globular clusters (Palomar 13 + AM 4) with UFCS-like properties.

A Dual-Hemisphere Spectroscopic Campaign: 215 Spectroscopic Members, 19 UFCSs

We succesfully targeted 21 UFCSs with the Keck/DEIMOS and Magellan/IMACS medium-resolution spectrographs (~2/3 the known population). We recover 215 spectroscopic members across 19 UFCSs, with a median of 9 member stars per system.

Key Result: The UFCSs are Kinematically Cold

We measure the velocity dispersions of 15 of our 19 UFCSs from samples of $\geq 5$ stars. In 10 of these 15 cases, we recover only upper limits, demonstrating that the UFCSs are kinematically cold systems. The remaining 5 cases displayed resolved dispersions up to $\sigma_v = 4$~km/s.

Key Result: The UFCSs are Chemically Diverse

We find that the UFCSs span a remarkably wide range in mean metallicity, ranging from $-3.3 \lesssim [Fe/H] -3.3 \lesssim -0.8$.
